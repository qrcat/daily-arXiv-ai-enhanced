### [71] [Characterizing and Recognizing Twistedness](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.16178)
*Oswin Aichholzer,Alfredo García,Javier Tejel,Birgit Vogtenhuber,Alexandra Weinberger*

Main category: cs.CG

TL;DR: 该论文提出了广义扭曲绘图的组合特征化方法，通过五顶点子旋转系统的局部性质来全局判断广义扭曲性，并开发了高效的识别算法。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 广义扭曲绘图最初用于改进完全图简单绘图中平面子结构大小的界限，但由于其几何定义复杂且依赖于具体绘图，需要开发纯组合的特征化方法来简化识别和理解。

Method: 通过分析抽象旋转系统的局部性质，证明对于n≥7的完全图，广义扭曲性等价于所有五顶点诱导的子旋转系统都是广义扭曲的。基于此开发了O(n⁵)时间算法，并进一步利用顶点对旋转特征开发了O(n²)时间算法。

Result: 获得了广义扭曲绘图的组合特征化定理，证明了局部五顶点条件足以保证全局广义扭曲性，并实现了高效的识别算法。

Conclusion: 该研究为广义扭曲绘图提供了纯组合的视角，简化了识别过程，深化了对简单绘图结构特征的理解，并为相关组合几何问题提供了新的工具。

Abstract: In a simple drawing of a graph, any two edges intersect in at most one point
(either a common endpoint or a proper crossing). A simple drawing is
generalized twisted if it fulfills certain rather specific constraints on how
the edges are drawn. An abstract rotation system of a graph assigns to each
vertex a cyclic order of its incident edges. A realizable rotation system is
one that admits a simple drawing such that at each vertex, the edges emanate in
that cyclic order, and a generalized twisted rotation system can be realized as
a generalized twisted drawing. Generalized twisted drawings have initially been
introduced to obtain improved bounds on the size of plane substructures in any
simple drawing of $K_n$. They have since gained independent interest due to
their surprising properties. However, the definition of generalized twisted
drawings is very geometric and drawing-specific.
  In this paper, we develop characterizations of generalized twisted drawings
that enable a purely combinatorial view on these drawings and lead to efficient
recognition algorithms. Concretely, we show that for any $n \geq 7$, an
abstract rotation system of $K_n$ is generalized twisted if and only if all
subrotation systems induced by five vertices are generalized twisted. This
implies a drawing-independent and concise characterization of generalized
twistedness. Besides, the result yields a simple $O(n^5)$-time algorithm to
decide whether an abstract rotation system is generalized twisted and sheds new
light on the structural features of simple drawings. We further develop a
characterization via the rotations of a pair of vertices in a drawing, which we
then use to derive an $O(n^2)$-time algorithm to decide whether a realizable
rotation system is generalized twisted.
